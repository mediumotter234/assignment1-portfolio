Personas & Storyboarding: Laundry Machines

Fall 2022, CS1300 Assignment

CONTEXT

I stepped into a user's shoes by observing real users interacting with the shared laundry machines in my dorm, developing personas and generating a storyboard based off my observations.

OBSERVATIONS

A few of my key observations:

  • All users tried to press the sliding tray in at least two times before they heard the rattling sound of the quarters falling into the machine.
  • Multiple users closed and opened the lid of the washer to get another look at the instructions.
  • All users hesitated when setting a timer on their phones.

QUESTIONS

I ased the following questions to users of the interface:

  • Do you feel like you used the interface as intended?
  • Did you find the instructions for use of the washer easy to find and understand?
  • Did you feel like there was enough relevant information presented on the interface?
  • How will you decide what time youâ€™ll return to the machine?
  • Rank the following in order of importance for you for this interface: ease of use, quality of result, price.

RESPONSES

Summaries of the responses to the questions above:

  • Users felt they used the interface as intended and instructions were easy to understand, but took issue with how the instructions were presented.
  • All the users said the cycle status panel would have been more helpful if they knew roughly how long each step usually took.
  • All the users said they had to guess based on previous experiences for how long the washing would take.
  • Two users said the process of using the machine took longer than they expected.
  • All the users ranked price last in importance, with quality of result and ease of use varying between first and second most important.
